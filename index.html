Spatial History of New Hampshire

A digital exploration of the Granite State’s past.

Many people associate the United States with California, New York, or Florida — regions that dominate global imagination and media landscapes. This project intentionally shifts attention toward a state that is far less prominent, yet historically rich and geographically distinctive: New Hampshire.

Spatial history examines how people interact with space — how borders, landscapes, mobility, settlement patterns, and environments shape human experience. We navigate space every day: through towns, along roads, across regions, and through the geographies that define our lives.

This website presents a multi‑module exploration of New Hampshire’s past through maps, GIS analysis, and narrative interpretation. Each module focuses on a different aspect of the state’s historical geography.
