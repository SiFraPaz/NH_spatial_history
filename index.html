Spatial History of New Hampshire

A digital exploration of the Granite State’s past.

Many people associate the United States with California, New York, or Florida — regions that dominate global imagination and media landscapes. This project intentionally shifts attention toward a state that is far less prominent, yet historically rich and geographically distinctive: New Hampshire.

Spatial history examines how people interact with space — how borders, landscapes, mobility, settlement patterns, and environments shape human experience.

This website presents a multi‑module exploration of New Hampshire’s past through maps, GIS analysis, and narrative interpretation.

Settlement & Colonial Boundaries

How colonial town grants and early land divisions shaped New Hampshire.

Indigenous Geographies

Native land, mobility, early contact history and spatial patterns.

Demography & Migration

Population landscapes and long‑term demographic change.

Industry & Economy

The role of rivers, mills, and industrial corridors in shaping the state.

Environmental Change

Ecology, land‑use, and conservation history.
